Round 2 — Consolidated top candidates

Best-of synthesis

Three options that combine the strongest ideas from across the original 18: Codex #1's clean triplet labels, Gemini #2's directional opacity ramp, Gemini #3's floating value bubble, Claude #1's monospace identity, Claude #2's motion-as-direction, and Codex #3's physical curves. Picks span the design space — most legible (A), most identity-preserving (B), most distinctive (C).

A. Polished Range Rail

Codex #1's clean track + Gemini #3's floating value bubble + Gemini #2's opacity-ramp direction cue. The "now" number rides on the thumb; the gradient encodes direction in the bar itself (tide fades toward the active edge as it drains, current intensifies toward the active edge as it builds). No animation needed.

B. Instrument Drift Bar

Keeps the design doc's monospace/instrument accent (Claude #1) and fixes every legibility issue: solid block glyphs, a bold colored "now" marker, the same opacity-ramp direction cue as A applied to the text (Gemini #2), plus a small pulsing arrow showing motion (Claude #2). The arrow still points the right way under prefers-reduced-motion — animation just stops.
